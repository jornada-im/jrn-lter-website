Cross-sectional area of creosote as a function of biomass
=========================================================

From Ludwig et al. (1975), table 2, aboveground volume and spatial area
of an individual is given in terms of biomass, from regressions on \\
eld measurements. The intent here is to get the cross-sectional area as
a function of biomass.

ChatGTP Evaluation
------------------

Starting from your definitions,

We are given the following equations:

#. :math:`X = r \cdot h`

#. :math:`\frac{B}{m} = \frac{\pi}{3} \cdot h \cdot r^2`

#. :math:`\sqrt{\frac{B}{n}} = \pi \cdot r^2`

with constants:

- :math:`n = 1504`

- :math:`m = 5836`

Our goal is to express :math:`X` as a function of :math:`B`.

Step 1: Solve Equation (3) for :math:`r^2`
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

From Equation (3):

.. math:: \sqrt{\frac{B}{n}} = \pi \cdot r^2

Squaring both sides:

.. math:: \frac{B}{n} = \pi^2 \cdot r^4

Solving for :math:`r^4`:

.. math:: r^4 = \frac{B}{n \cdot \pi^2}

Step 2: Solve Equation (2) for :math:`h`
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

From Equation (2):

.. math:: \frac{B}{m} = \frac{\pi}{3} \cdot h \cdot r^2

Solving for :math:`h`:

.. math:: h = \frac{3 B}{m \cdot \pi \cdot r^2}

Step 3: Substitute :math:`h` into Equation (1)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

From Equation (1):

.. math:: X = r \cdot h

Substitute the expression for :math:`h`:

.. math::

   X = r \cdot \left( \frac{3 B}{m \cdot \pi \cdot r^2} \right) = \frac{3 B}{m
      \cdot \pi \cdot r}

Step 4: Express :math:`r` in Terms of :math:`B`
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

From Step 1:

.. math:: r^4 = \frac{B}{n \cdot \pi^2}

Taking the fourth root:

.. math:: r = \left( \frac{B}{n \cdot \pi^2} \right)^{1 / 4}

Step 5: Substitute :math:`r` into the Expression for :math:`X`
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

.. math::

   X = \frac{3 B}{m \cdot \pi \cdot \left( \frac{B}{n \cdot \pi^2} \right)^{1
      / 4}}

Simplify the expression:

.. math::

   X = \frac{3 B}{m \cdot \pi} \cdot \left( \frac{n \cdot \pi^2}{B} \right)^{1
      / 4}

.. math::

   X = \frac{3 B}{m \cdot \pi} \cdot (n^{1 / 4} \cdot \pi^{1 / 2} \cdot B^{- 1
      / 4})

.. math:: X = \frac{3 \cdot n^{1 / 4} \cdot \pi^{1 / 2} \cdot B^{3 / 4}}{m \cdot \pi}

Simplify :math:`\pi^{1 / 2} / \pi = \pi^{- 1 / 2}`:

.. math:: X = \frac{3 \cdot n^{1 / 4} \cdot B^{3 / 4}}{m \cdot \pi^{1 / 2}}

Step 6: Plug in the Values for :math:`n` and :math:`m`
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Given:

- :math:`n = 1504`

- :math:`m = 5836`

Compute :math:`n^{1 / 4}`:

.. math:: n^{1 / 4} = 1504^{1 / 4} \approx 6.2

Compute :math:`\pi^{1 / 2}`:

.. math:: \pi^{1 / 2} \approx \sqrt{\pi} \approx 1.772

Now, plug in the values:

.. math:: X \approx \frac{3 \cdot 6.2 \cdot B^{3 / 4}}{5836 \cdot 1.772}

.. math:: X \approx \frac{18.6 \cdot B^{3 / 4}}{10345.792}

.. math:: X \approx 0.0018 \cdot B^{3 / 4}

Final Answer:
~~~~~~~~~~~~~

.. math:: X \approx 0.0018 \cdot B^{3 / 4}

This expresses :math:`X` as a function of :math:`B`, given the provided
equations and constants.
